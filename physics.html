Electrostatics

Mechanics is the branch of physics dealing with the motion of objects and the forces that act upon them. It includes classical mechanics and quantum mechanics.

Key Concepts

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Principles describing the relationship between an object's motion and the forces acting on it.
  • Work and Energy: Concepts related to the capacity to perform work and the transformation of energy.
  • Momentum: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its motion.

Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics studies heat, energy, and work. It explains how energy is transferred and transformed in physical systems.

Key Concepts

  • First Law of Thermodynamics: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
  • Second Law of Thermodynamics: Entropy in an isolated system tends to increase over time.
  • Third Law of Thermodynamics: Entropy approaches a constant value as temperature approaches absolute zero.

Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism covers electric and magnetic fields and their interactions with matter. It includes phenomena like electricity, magnetism, and electromagnetic waves.

Key Concepts

  • Electric Fields: Areas around charged particles where electric forces are felt.
  • Magnetic Fields: Regions where magnetic forces are exerted by magnets or moving charges.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Waves that propagate through space carrying electromagnetic energy.

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum Mechanics explores the behavior of particles on the atomic and subatomic levels, focusing on wave-particle duality and quantization of energy.

Key Concepts

  • Wave-Particle Duality: Particles exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties.
  • Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: It's impossible to precisely measure both position and momentum simultaneously.
  • Quantum Entanglement: Particles can instantaneously affect each other's state, regardless of distance.

Optics

Optics is the study of light and its interactions with matter, including phenomena like reflection, refraction, and dispersion.

Key Concepts

  • Reflection: The bouncing back of light rays when they hit a surface.
  • Refraction: The bending of light as it passes from one medium to another.
  • Diffraction: The spreading of light waves around obstacles and openings.

Relativity

Relativity is a theory developed by Albert Einstein, encompassing special relativity and general relativity, which describe the relationship between space, time, and gravity.

Key Concepts

  • Special Relativity: Describes the relationship between space and time in the absence of gravity.
  • General Relativity: Describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

Nuclear Physics

Nuclear Physics focuses on the study of atomic nuclei, nuclear reactions, and the forces that hold the nucleus together.

Key Concepts

  • Nuclear Fission: The splitting of a heavy nucleus into lighter nuclei, releasing energy.
  • Nuclear Fusion: The combining of light nuclei to form heavier nuclei, releasing energy.
  • Radioactivity: The process by which unstable atomic nuclei lose energy by emitting radiation.

Astrophysics

Astrophysics applies the principles of physics to understand the behavior and properties of celestial objects and phenomena.

Key Concepts

  • Stellar Evolution: The life cycle of stars from formation to death.
  • Black Holes: Regions of spacetime with gravitational fields so strong that nothing can escape from them.
  • Cosmology: The study of the origin, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe.

Fluid Dynamics

Fluid Dynamics is the study of fluids (liquids and gases) and their interactions with forces and boundaries.

Key Concepts

  • Continuity Equation: Describes the conservation of mass in fluid flow.
  • Bernoulli's Principle: Relates the pressure, velocity, and height in a moving fluid.
  • Navier-Stokes Equations: Describe the motion of viscous fluid substances.

Statistical Mechanics

Statistical Mechanics provides a framework for relating the microscopic properties of particles to the macroscopic observable properties of materials.

Key Concepts

  • Boltzmann Distribution: Describes the distribution of particles over various energy states in a system.
  • Partition Function: A function that summarizes the statistical properties of a system in thermodynamic equilibrium.
  • Entropy: A measure of the disorder or randomness in a system.

Condensed Matter Physics

Condensed Matter Physics studies the physical properties of condensed phases of matter, including solids and liquids.

Key Concepts

  • Crystal Structure: The ordered arrangement of atoms in a crystalline solid.
  • Superconductivity: A phenomenon where a material can conduct electricity without resistance below a certain temperature.
  • Semiconductors: Materials with electrical conductivity between that of conductors and insulators, used in electronic devices.

Plasma Physics

Plasma Physics studies ionized gases, or plasmas, which are made up of positively charged ions and free electrons.

Key Concepts

  • Plasma State: The fourth state of matter, characterized by ionized particles.
  • Magnetic Confinement: A method to confine plasma using magnetic fields, used in fusion reactors.
  • Space Plasmas: Plasmas found in space environments, such as the solar wind and interstellar medium.
