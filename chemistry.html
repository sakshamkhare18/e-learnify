solution

A solution is a homogeneous mixture of one or more solutes dissolved in a solvent.

Key Concepts

  • liquid solution: A liquid solution is a homogenous mixture of two or more substances.
  • Gaseous solution: The solution in which the solvent is a gas is called a gaseous solution.

Electrochemistry

Electrochemistry is the study of electron movement in an oxidation or reduction reaction at a polarized electrode surface.

Key Concepts

  • Nernst equation : Nernst equation is responsible for providing the relation between the cell potential of an electrochemical cell.
  • Reference Electrode: Reference Electrode. If the electrode of the potential is known, then it is called as the reference electrode

Chemical Kinetics

chemical kinetics, the branch of physical chemistry that is concerned with understanding the rates of chemical reactions.

Key Concepts

  • Synthesis: Synthesis is a form of analysis related to comparison and contrast, classification and division.
  • tha rate of reaction: The reaction rate or rate of reaction is the speed at which a chemical reaction takes place.

d -and f -Block Elements

The d-block of the periodic table contains the elements of the groups 3-12 in which the d orbitals are progressively filled in each of the four long periods .

Key Concepts

Coordination Compounds

coordination compound, any of a class of substances with chemical structures in which a central metal atom is surrounded by nonmetal atoms or groups of atoms, called ligands, joined to it by chemical bonds.

Key Concepts

  • ligand: ligand is an ion or molecule with a functional group that binds to a central metal atom to form a coordination complex..
  • Types of Ligands: The ligands can be classified into three types: simple, complex, and coordinative unsaturation.
